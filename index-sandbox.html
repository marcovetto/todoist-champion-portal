Champion Portal · Module 1

Todoist Fundamentals

Understand what Todoist is, how it's structured, and how to set up a workspace for your team.

1.1 — What is Todoist?

→

A task manager built for teams

Todoist helps individuals and teams capture, organise, and act on their work — across all devices. The Business plan adds workspace-level collaboration, task assignment, and admin controls.

→

Two workspaces in one account

Every user has My Projects (personal) and their team workspace. Both are accessible from the same sidebar.

1.2 — Workspace Setup

1

Create your workspace

Go to todoist.com → click your workspace name → Create or join a workspace. Give it your company or team name.

2

Invite team members

From workspace Settings → Members, invite by email. Assign admin or member roles.

3

Organise with folders

Click workspace name → Add folder. Group related projects (e.g., "Marketing Campaigns", "Operations").

Champion tip

Set up a "Training" project before your onboarding session — a sandbox where team members can practice hands-on without affecting real work.

1.3 — The Interface

→

Left sidebar

Contains Today, Upcoming, filters, labels, and all projects. The workspace section lives at the bottom.

→

Available on all platforms

Web, macOS, Windows, iOS, Android. Experience is consistent. Keyboard shortcuts vary slightly on mobile.

Core Features

A deep dive into the features your team will use every day.

2.1 — Tasks

1

Quick Add (Q)

Press Q anywhere to open Quick Add. Todoist parses dates, priorities, and labels from natural language automatically.

2

Natural language

Examples: Report due friday 3pm p1, Team sync every monday at 9am, Follow up in 3 days.

3

Priorities

P1 (red) → P2 (orange) → P3 (blue) → P4 (default). Agree on a team-wide priority convention.

4

Recurring tasks

Type every monday, every 2 weeks, every month on the 1st. Completing one instance auto-schedules the next.

2.2 — Projects & Sections

1

Create a project

Click + next to workspace name → Add project → name, layout (List or Board), colour.

2

Add sections

Three-dot menu → Add section. Use "To Do / In Progress / Done" or a workflow specific to your team.

3

Share the project

Share icon (top right) → enter email → Invite. Members can join immediately.

2.3 — Labels & Filters

→

Labels

Add labels to tasks across projects (e.g., @client, @urgent, @waiting). Use them to build powerful cross-project views.

→

Custom filters

Filters & Labels → + → define a query. Examples: today & assigned to: me, p1 & overdue, @waiting.

Collaboration & Teams

How to use the Business workspace to collaborate effectively.

3.1 — Workspace Management

→

Business plan features

Unlimited projects, team inbox, Reporting (Activity Log), admin controls, and priority support.

→

Admin controls

Manage who can create projects, invite members, and view workspace activity. Settings → Members.

3.2 — Assignment & Comments

1

Assign a task

While creating: press + and type a name. On existing tasks: hover → assignee icon → select from dropdown.

2

Comments & @mentions

Click the speech bubble on any task. Type @name to notify. Attach files or links to keep context in one place.

3.3 — Integrations

→

Email to task

Forward emails to your Todoist inbox address to create tasks automatically.

→

Calendar sync

Connect Google Calendar or Outlook. Settings → Integrations → Calendar.

→

Slack

Create tasks from Slack messages and receive Todoist notifications in channels.

→

Advanced & Best Practices

Workflow design, templates, productivity tracking, and troubleshooting.

4.1 — Workflow Design

→

Start with real workflows

Map out 3–4 recurring workflows your team actually does (e.g., client onboarding, sprint planning, content publishing) and model each as a project template.

→

Project templates

Build a model project, then duplicate it for new initiatives. Browse ready-made templates at todoist.com/templates.

→

Naming conventions

Agree team-wide on naming for projects, labels, and sections. Consistency makes filters much more powerful.

4.2 — Reporting & Tracking

→

Reporting (Activity Log)

Shows all completed tasks across the workspace. Filter by user, project, and date. Use it to spot adoption gaps or track output.

→

Karma

Tracks personal task completion streaks. Optional but useful as a motivator for individuals during onboarding.

4.3 — Troubleshooting

→

Common issues

  • Tasks not syncing: close and reopen the app, check connection
  • Missing tasks: check Reporting — tasks may be completed, not deleted
  • Notification overload: adjust per-project notification settings

Running Training Sessions

Everything you need to deliver effective onboarding for your team.

Trainer tip

The best sessions are hands-on. Use the Interactive Demos as live walkthroughs — let participants click through them on their own screens.

How to run your first session

1

Set up before the session

Create a shared "Training" project in the workspace as a sandbox for practice.

2

Share the onboarding guide in advance

Send the PDF (Resources section) 24h before. Ask participants to create an account and join the workspace first.

3

Walk through the demos live

Use the "Create folders" and "Create a filter" demos in Module 2. Let participants follow on their own screens.

4

Focus on 3–4 core workflows

Don't try to cover everything. Tailor to what your team will actually use daily. Depth over breadth.

5

End with a real task

Ask everyone to add one real work task in Todoist before the session closes. Nothing reinforces training like doing it for real.

6

Schedule a follow-up

A 30-minute check-in 1 week later makes a big difference. Adoption drops without a follow-up touchpoint.

Session formats

Quick Start (30 min)

Account setup, adding tasks, joining the workspace. Best for time-constrained teams.

Practice Mode

Select an article to read it here, then follow the guided steps in Todoist.

Exercise
Create your first filter
Build a filter that shows all P1 tasks due today assigned to you. Follow the steps, then try it in the demo on the right.
Target query

today & p1 & assigned to: me

1
Open Filters & Labels

Click Filters & Labels in the left sidebar of Todoist.

2
Add a new filter

Click the + icon next to Filters to open the Add filter window.

3
Name your filter

Type My urgent tasks today in the Name field.

4
Enter the query

In the Query field, type:

today & p1 & assigned to: me

today — due today  ·  & — AND  ·  p1 — priority 1  ·  assigned to: me — your tasks only
5
Save the filter

Click Add. The filter now appears under Filters & Labels in your sidebar.

6
Test it

Click the filter to see all P1 tasks due today that are assigned to you.

💡 Want to go further? Try (today | overdue) & p1 to also catch overdue P1 tasks.

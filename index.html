Your first week · Lesson 1

Get to know Todoist

Take a tour of the interface in the sandbox on the right. No account needed yet — just click around.

Goal
Know where the main pieces of the UI live
Before you sign in to your real account, get comfortable with the layout. It's the same everywhere.
1
Scan the left sidebar

Find the main navigation: Inbox, Today, Upcoming, Filters & Labels. These are the views you'll use every day.

2
Click Inbox

Your capture space. Any task you add without specifying a project lands here.

3
Click Today

Your daily shortlist — everything due today, sorted by time and priority.

4
Click Upcoming

The next 7 days at a glance. You'll come back here in Lesson 3.

5
Click Filters & Labels

Saved searches. You'll build one in Lesson 5 to surface your P1 work across every project.

6
Find the Projects section

Below the main views — this is where personal projects live. In your real account, your team's shared projects will sit here too.

Two workspaces in one account

In your real account you'll see two workspaces in the sidebar: My Projects (personal) and your team workspace (shared). The sandbox shows only personal mode — the team workspace appears once you're logged in with your real team.

Ready to join your team for real?

Check your email for an invite from your champion, or head to todoist.com and log in. Then pick up with Lesson 2 — your changes in this sandbox don't affect your real account.

You know your way around. Time to start doing.

Your first week · Lesson 2

Add & complete tasks

Practice in the live sandbox on the right. No safety rails — your changes stay in your browser and reset on reload.

Goal
Add a task with natural language, then complete it
Capture work fast, set a priority, tick it off. This is 90% of daily Todoist.
1
Open Inbox or any project

Click + Add task, or press Q from anywhere to open Quick Add.

2
Try natural language

Type this exactly:

Draft the onboarding brief fri 3pm p1

Watch Todoist parse it — fri 3pm becomes Friday at 3 PM, p1 sets priority 1 (red).
3
Add a description (optional)

Click beneath the task name to add context or links.

4
Save it

Click Add task — it lands in your list.

5
Complete it

Click the circle to the left of the task. It disappears into your Activity Log.

Changed your mind? Click Undo immediately to bring it back.
6
Try a recurring task

Add:

Team sync every monday at 9am

Completing a recurring task auto-schedules the next instance.
Nailed it — you've got the Todoist basics.

Your first week · Lesson 3

Plan your day

Explore the three planning views in the sandbox on the right. Move tasks around — nothing sticks.

Goal
Find your way around Today, Upcoming, and Calendar
Three views, one daily habit: open Today, glance at Upcoming, drag the overflow.
1
Open Today

Click Today in the sidebar. Everything due today lives here, sorted by time and priority.

This is where your morning starts. Two minutes here beats an hour reacting later.
2
Open Upcoming

Click Upcoming to see the next 7 days at a glance.

3
Drag to reschedule

Grab any task in Upcoming and drop it on another day — Todoist updates the due date instantly.

4
Switch to Calendar view

In Upcoming, click View → Calendar.

5
Try weekly vs. monthly

Flip between the two layouts to see which matches how you think about your week.

You've got a planning habit. Start every morning in Today.

Your first week · Lesson 4

Collaborate with your team

Find the collaboration controls in the sandbox on the right. You'll use them for real once you're in your team's workspace.

Goal
Know where assignment, comments, and @mentions live
The sandbox has no real teammates, but every collaboration control is here. Find them so you're not hunting around on day one.
1
Open a task

Click any task name in the sandbox to open its detail view.

2
Find the assignee button

Look for a person-silhouette icon near the due date. In a real shared project, this is where you pick who owns the task.

You can also assign while creating: press + in the task editor and type a name.
3
Open comments

Click the speech-bubble icon to open the comment thread on the task.

4
Try writing a comment

Post an update, paste a link, or drag in a file. Everything stays tied to the task.

5
Try @mention

Type @ in a comment. The mention dropdown appears — in your real workspace, this is how you ping a teammate.

The sandbox has no teammates to tag, so the dropdown will be empty. That's expected.
You know where everything lives. One lesson to go.

Your first week · Lesson 5

Find what matters with filters

A filter is a saved search across every project. Build one, reuse it forever.

Goal
Build a filter that shows your P1 tasks
One custom view that surfaces every priority-1 task across all your projects. Do it in the sandbox on the right.
Common filter queries

today & assigned to: me · your day only.
p1 · every top-priority task.
overdue & assigned to: me · catch-up mode.
@waiting · anything labelled "waiting."

1
Open Filters & Labels

Click Filters & Labels in the left sidebar of the sandbox.

2
Add a new filter

Click the + icon next to Filters to open the Add-filter window.

3
Name it

Type My P1 tasks in the Name field.

4
Enter the query

In the Query field, type:

p1

p1 — priority 1, the highest priority level in Todoist (shown in red).
5
Save

Click Add. The filter appears under Filters & Labels in the sidebar.

6
Test it

Click the filter to see all your P1 tasks gathered in a single view.

💡 Want to go further? Try p1 & today to narrow to P1 tasks due today.
You've finished your first week with Todoist.

Champion Portal · Start

Define your why

Adoption sticks when people know why change matters. Before setting anything up, capture the reason your team needs Todoist.

⏱ ~5 min By the end: a one-page rollout plan you can share with your team.

Answer four questions

1

What problem are we solving?

Missed deadlines? Work scattered across email, Slack, and docs? Unclear priorities? Write it in one sentence.

2

Who is the rollout for?

How many people, which teams, and who's likely to be skeptical? Plan for the skeptics — they're your real measure of success.

3

What does success look like in 30 / 60 / 90 days?

Examples: 80% of the team logs in weekly; all sprint tasks live in Todoist; no more "where is that task?" messages.

4

What's our timeline?

Pick a date for the kickoff session, a check-in one week later, and a 30-day review.

Champion tip

Share your answers with your manager before the rollout starts. A two-minute alignment conversation now prevents a two-week detour later.

Champion Portal · Step 1

Set up your workspace

Understand what Todoist is, how it's structured, and how to set up a workspace your team can join.

⏱ ~10 min By the end: a workspace ready for your team — folders, members, and a training sandbox.

1.1 — What is Todoist?

→

A task manager built for teams

Todoist helps individuals and teams capture, organise, and act on their work — across all devices. The Business plan adds workspace-level collaboration, task assignment, and admin controls.

→

Two workspaces in one account

Every user has My Projects (personal) and their team workspace. Both are accessible from the same sidebar.

1.2 — Workspace Setup

1

Create your workspace

Go to todoist.com → click your workspace name → Create or join a workspace. Give it your company or team name.

2

Invite team members

From workspace Settings → Members, invite by email. Assign admin or member roles.

3

Organise with folders

Click workspace name → Add folder. Group related projects (e.g., "Marketing Campaigns", "Operations").

Champion tip

Set up a "Training" project before your onboarding session — a sandbox where team members can practice hands-on without affecting real work.

1.3 — The Interface

→

Left sidebar

Contains Today, Upcoming, filters, labels, and all projects. The workspace section lives at the bottom.

→

Available on all platforms

Web, macOS, Windows, iOS, Android. Experience is consistent. Keyboard shortcuts vary slightly on mobile.

Next: Design your workflows

Champion Portal · Step 2

Design your workflows

Tasks, projects, labels, and filters — the building blocks for modelling the work your team actually does.

⏱ ~15 min By the end: you can model your team's real work and find it again with filters.

2.1 — Tasks

1

Quick Add (Q)

Press Q anywhere to open Quick Add. Todoist parses dates, priorities, and labels from natural language automatically.

2

Natural language

Examples: Report due friday 3pm p1, Team sync every monday at 9am, Follow up in 3 days.

3

Priorities

P1 (red) → P2 (orange) → P3 (blue) → P4 (default). Agree on a team-wide priority convention.

4

Recurring tasks

Type every monday, every 2 weeks, every month on the 1st. Completing one instance auto-schedules the next.

2.2 — Projects & Sections

1

Create a project

Click + next to workspace name → Add project → name, layout (List or Board), colour.

2

Add sections

Three-dot menu → Add section. Use "To Do / In Progress / Done" or a workflow specific to your team.

3

Share the project

Share icon (top right) → enter email → Invite. Members can join immediately.

2.3 — Labels & Filters

→

Labels

Add labels to tasks across projects (e.g., @client, @urgent, @waiting). Use them to build powerful cross-project views.

→

Custom filters

Filters & Labels → + → define a query. Examples: today & assigned to: me, p1 & overdue, @waiting.

Champion Portal · Step 3

Enable your team

Assignments, comments, and integrations — how to make Todoist the place your team actually collaborates.

⏱ ~10 min By the end: you can assign work, keep context in one place, and connect the tools your team already uses.

3.1 — Workspace Management

→

Business plan features

Unlimited projects, team inbox, Reporting (Activity Log), admin controls, and priority support.

→

Admin controls

Manage who can create projects, invite members, and view workspace activity. Settings → Members.

3.2 — Assignment & Comments

1

Assign a task

While creating: press + and type a name. On existing tasks: hover → assignee icon → select from dropdown.

2

Comments & @mentions

Click the speech bubble on any task. Type @name to notify. Attach files or links to keep context in one place.

3.3 — Integrations

→

Email to task

Forward emails to your Todoist inbox address to create tasks automatically.

→

Calendar sync

Connect Google Calendar or Outlook. Settings → Integrations → Calendar.

→

Slack

Create tasks from Slack messages and receive Todoist notifications in channels.

Next: Measure & sustain adoption

Champion Portal · Step 4

Measure & sustain adoption

Templates, reporting, and troubleshooting — keep momentum past the kickoff week.

⏱ ~10 min By the end: you can track adoption, reuse what works, and handle the most common questions.

4.1 — Workflow Design

→

Start with real workflows

Map out 3–4 recurring workflows your team actually does (e.g., client onboarding, sprint planning, content publishing) and model each as a project template.

→

Project templates

Build a model project, then duplicate it for new initiatives. Browse ready-made templates at todoist.com/templates.

→

Naming conventions

Agree team-wide on naming for projects, labels, and sections. Consistency makes filters much more powerful.

4.2 — Reporting & Tracking

→

Reporting (Activity Log)

Shows all completed tasks across the workspace. Filter by user, project, and date. Use it to spot adoption gaps or track output.

→

Karma

Tracks personal task completion streaks. Optional but useful as a motivator for individuals during onboarding.

4.3 — Troubleshooting

→

Common issues

  • Tasks not syncing: close and reopen the app, check connection
  • Missing tasks: check Reporting — tasks may be completed, not deleted
  • Notification overload: adjust per-project notification settings

Running Training Sessions

Everything you need to deliver effective onboarding for your team.

Trainer tip

The best sessions are hands-on. Use the Interactive Demos as live walkthroughs — let participants click through them on their own screens.

How to run your first session

1

Set up before the session

Create a shared "Training" project in the workspace as a sandbox for practice.

2

Share the onboarding guide in advance

Send the PDF (Resources section) 24h before. Ask participants to create an account and join the workspace first.

3

Walk through the demos live

Use the "Create folders" and "Create a filter" demos in Module 2. Let participants follow on their own screens.

4

Focus on 3–4 core workflows

Don't try to cover everything. Tailor to what your team will actually use daily. Depth over breadth.

5

End with a real task

Ask everyone to add one real work task in Todoist before the session closes. Nothing reinforces training like doing it for real.

6

Schedule a follow-up

A 30-minute check-in 1 week later makes a big difference. Adoption drops without a follow-up touchpoint.

Session formats

⚡

Quick Start (30 min)

Account setup, adding tasks, joining the workspace. Best for time-constrained teams.

Beginner
🎯

Core Training (90 min)

Full feature overview with hands-on practice. Tasks, projects, collaboration, filters.

Standard
🏋️

Deep Dive (half day)

For Champions and admins. Workflow design, templates, integrations.
